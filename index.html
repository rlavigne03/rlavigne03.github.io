My Future Career Plans

With the educational path I`m on, I plan to continue going higher. An Associate of Science and a Bachelors in Biology, I plan on getting my Masters of Genetic Counseling. Genetic Counselors work in places such as hospitals, private practices and research facilities. They help you understand your genetic risks based on your family history, genetic risks for certain diseases or cancer, whether genetic testing might be right for you, and what the results mean.

Genetic Counselors have training in medical genetics and counseling to guide and support patients seeking information about how inherited diseases might affect them, and to interpret genetic test results based on family history. They meet with individuals before or after genetic testing. They are specialized in areas like prenatal, pediatric, oncology, and psychiatry. As well as meeting with patients they can work in research, education, and industry. Also Genetic Counseling is a growing field with more and more opportunities coming with it.
